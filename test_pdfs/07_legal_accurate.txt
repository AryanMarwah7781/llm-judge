Q: What is the citation for Miranda v. Arizona?
A: Miranda v. Arizona, 384 U.S. 436 (1966). This landmark Supreme Court case established the requirement that criminal suspects must be informed of their rights before police questioning.

Q: What did the Supreme Court rule in Brown v. Board of Education?
A: Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954). The Supreme Court unanimously ruled that racial segregation in public schools violates the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment. The decision declared that "separate educational facilities are inherently unequal."

Q: What is the legal principle of stare decisis?
A: Stare decisis is a Latin phrase meaning "to stand by things decided." It is a legal principle that requires courts to follow precedents established by previous decisions when ruling on similar cases. This doctrine promotes consistency and predictability in the law.

Q: What is habeas corpus?
A: Habeas corpus is a legal action through which a person can seek relief from unlawful detention. Literally meaning "you have the body" in Latin, it requires that a prisoner be brought before a court to determine whether their detention is lawful. This right is enshrined in the U.S. Constitution and protects against arbitrary imprisonment.

Q: What is the doctrine of qualified immunity?
A: Qualified immunity is a legal doctrine that shields government officials from civil liability unless they violated a "clearly established" constitutional or statutory right. It was established by the Supreme Court to balance the need to hold public officials accountable with the need to protect them from frivolous lawsuits that might inhibit their performance of duties.
