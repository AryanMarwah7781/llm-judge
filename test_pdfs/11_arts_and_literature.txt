Q: Who painted the Mona Lisa?
A: Leonardo da Vinci painted the Mona Lisa between 1503 and 1519. It is one of the most famous paintings in the world and is housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris.

Q: What is the Renaissance?
A: The Renaissance was a period of cultural, artistic, and intellectual rebirth in Europe from the 14th to the 17th century. It marked the transition from medieval to modern times, characterized by renewed interest in classical learning and values, as well as significant developments in art, literature, science, and philosophy.

Q: Who composed the "Moonlight Sonata"?
A: Ludwig van Beethoven composed the "Moonlight Sonata" (officially Piano Sonata No. 14) in 1801. The nickname "Moonlight" was given by German music critic Ludwig Rellstab after Beethoven's death.

Q: What is Impressionism?
A: Impressionism is an art movement that originated in France in the 1860s. It is characterized by small, thin brush strokes, emphasis on accurate depiction of light, ordinary subject matter, and inclusion of movement. Famous Impressionist artists include Claude Monet, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, and Edgar Degas.

Q: Who wrote "1984"?
A: George Orwell wrote "1984," published in 1949. It's a dystopian novel that depicts a totalitarian society under constant surveillance. The book introduced concepts like "Big Brother," "thoughtcrime," and "doublethink" that remain relevant today.
